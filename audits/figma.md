### Palette Strategy and Restraint/Richness

Figma's palette is **disciplined and restrained at the UI level, expressive and unpredictable at the content level.** The core product UI palette is essentially a four-color system: black, white, violet (for primary CTA), and green (for the logo mark). This extreme restraint makes the brand feel premium and professional. However, this is intentionally counterbalanced by an **unlimited, vibrant color freedom** expressed through product screenshots, user-made template thumbnails, blog illustration artwork, and the multicolor Figma logo mark itself. The brand says: "Our interface is clean so your work can be colorful." This is a semiotic masterstroke — Figma is a canvas, and the tool's UI recedes so the user's creativity can dominate.

### Color Application by Page Type

**Homepage:** Warm off-white hero, white main body, alternating lime-green (#DCFAAD) feature sections, dark black template section. Violet CTA in nav. Product screenshots introduce vivid color.

**Product Detail Pages (Figma Design, FigJam, Dev Mode, AI):** White background with product-specific illustration color introduced (magenta/purple for Dev Mode, blue-cyan/orange for AI, warm green for FigJam). Split-layout hero with illustration on right at full bleed.

**Pricing Page:** All-white with clear plan card grid, subtle borders, green "NEW" badge, minimal color injection. Lets pricing info breathe without visual noise.

**Enterprise Page:** White body with a dramatic **full-bleed black** illustration panel on the right half of the hero — a unique departure that signals premium, serious B2B credibility.

**Blog (Shortcut):** **Full black background** with multicolor paper-shape floating illustrations. Completely different visual language from product pages — signals editorial independence and playfulness.

**Contact Page:** **Sage green (#EAF5E4) background** — warm, approachable, reduces anxiety for a sales inquiry form. Smart emotional calibration.

**Customers Page:** **Cyan/teal logo strip** below the hero — signals confidence and community.

**Careers Page:** Body white with a **lavender/purple** section and real photography of diverse employees.

**Community Page:** White with a blue-to-pink gradient announcement banner. User-generated content dominates with vivid thumbnails.

### Color Hierarchy & Emotional Role Analysis

1. **Black (#000000):** Authority, power, precision — used for all primary actions, headings, wordmark
2. **White (#FFFFFF):** Space, clarity, openness — the dominant canvas color
3. **Violet (#5B57D1):** Energy, creativity, modernity — the one "brand color" in the traditional sense; used exclusively for the highest-priority CTAs
4. **Lime/mint green (#DCFAAD):** Warmth, productivity, approachability — used for feature modules
5. **Multicolor (Figma logo):** Inclusivity, creative breadth, playfulness — signals "we are for all creators"

### Colors NOT Used (Deliberate Absences)

- **No red/warning colors** in any UI state (pricing, nav, CTAs) — communicates safety and trust
- **No gradients on CTAs or backgrounds** (at the product marketing level) — signals straightforwardness
- **No pastels or "design agency" muted tones** in the primary UI
- **No orange as a primary brand color** (orange appears only in logo illustrations and user content)
- **No gold, navy, or corporate blue** — deliberately avoids traditional enterprise color coding
- **No dark mode toggle** on the marketing site — always light-mode (deliberate to keep UX simple and accessible)

## 2. Typography

### Font Families Identified

**Primary Font:** **"Inter"** — The dominant sans-serif typeface used throughout the entire Figma.com marketing site. Inter is a grotesque sans-serif designed specifically for screen interfaces by Rasmus Andersson. Its metrics are optimized for readability at small sizes while retaining geometric character at display sizes. Used for all body copy, UI labels, navigation, forms, and small-to-medium headings.

**Display/Heading Font (Product Pages):** The large H1 display headings on product pages (e.g., "Figma Design," "FigJam," "Dev Mode," "Figma AI is your creative collaborator") appear to use a **bold weight of Inter** at very large sizes (approximately 72–96px equivalent). The headline weight is extremely heavy — likely **Inter Black (900) or Extra Bold (800)**. The letter forms are characteristic of Inter at maximum weight.

**Blog ("Shortcut") Masthead Font:** The blog uses **"SHORTCUT"** as a display wordmark in what appears to be a **condensed, wide-tracked, geometric uppercase display font** — potentially a custom typeface or a display variant. The blog navigation uses a lighter weight sans-serif (likely Inter) for category links.

**Code/Monospace (Dev Mode page):** Dev Mode marketing pages show monospace code snippets in product screenshots using what appears to be **JetBrains Mono** or **Fira Code** (developer-facing fonts within the product UI, not the marketing site itself).

**No serif fonts are present anywhere on the site.** This is a deliberate choice that signals technical precision, modernity, and egalitarianism (serifs often signal tradition, hierarchy, or luxury).

### Typography Application by Page Type

**Homepage:** Uses the widest range of typographic sizes. H1 display text in the hero carousel is very large (80–96px), establishing Figma as visually bold and confident. Section H2s at ~52px maintain momentum. Body copy is 17–18px for readability.

**Product Detail Pages (Figma Design, FigJam, Dev Mode, AI):** H1 is even larger than homepage, sometimes spanning two lines at 72–80px to accommodate product names. Sub-descriptors at 17–18px explain functionality. Feature section H2s are approximately 36–44px.

**Pricing Page:** Typography becomes more utilitarian. Plan names are ~26px bold, prices are 32–40px for scanability. Feature comparison table uses 13–14px body text for density.

**Enterprise Page:** The eyebrow "FIGMA ENTERPRISE" uses ALL CAPS tracked text at ~12px. The H1 is split across two lines in very large type (~72px+), creating commanding visual weight.

**Blog (Shortcut):** Entirely different typographic system. "SHORTCUT" masthead in enormous condensed display. Article headline H2s at 18–22px in regular body weight. Category labels in ALL CAPS throughout. Much more editorial feel.

**Contact Page:** Form labels are in ALL CAPS tracking (a consistent brand pattern for form fields). Section heading "Contact us" is large display (roughly 52–64px), with subheading "Talk to a member of our sales team" in bold ~24px.

**Careers Page:** Large display heading "Careers at Figma" at ~64–72px. Values section uses H3 at ~28px.

**Community Page:** Different nav font (part of the community platform). H1 at ~40px uses standard Inter bold.

### Font Pairing Philosophy

Figma uses **zero pairings** — a mono-font system. This is itself a design statement of supreme confidence: Inter is versatile enough at all weights and sizes to carry the entire typographic hierarchy alone. This choice:
1. Signals **coherence and systems thinking** — appropriate for a design systems product
2. Reflects **Figma's engineering DNA** — Inter is the standard for product/developer interfaces
3. Avoids the "look how expressive we are" trap — restraint IS the message
4. Ensures **maximum legibility** across global audiences (Inter has excellent multilingual support)

The only exception is the blog's "SHORTCUT" display wordmark, which uses a display/custom face, signaling that the blog has its own creative sub-identity.

### Weight Distribution Analysis

- **Black / Ultra-Heavy (900):** Display H1 headlines only. Creates drama and authority.
- **ExtraBold (800):** H1 variants and some H2 where extra emphasis is needed.
- **Bold (700):** Most H2 section headings, plan names in pricing, feature section headings.
- **SemiBold (600):** H3, pricing prices, some nav elements, feature sub-headings.
- **Medium (500):** CTA button text, some UI labels.
- **Regular (400):** ALL body copy, nav links, footer, form labels, all paragraph text.
- **Light (300):** Rarely observed; possibly for testimonial body text or long-form content.

### Letter-Spacing Rules

- **Display H1 (large):** Tight tracking, approximately -0.02em to -0.04em — tightening large headlines is standard optical correction
- **ALL CAPS labels/eyebrows:** Wide tracking +0.08em to +0.12em — makes uppercase readable at small sizes
- **Body copy:** Normal tracking (0em)
- **CTA buttons:** Normal tracking
- **Footer headers:** Wide tracking consistent with ALL CAPS pattern

## 3. Logo & Wordmark

### Primary Logo Description

The **Figma logo** consists of two components used in tandem:
1. **The Figma Mark (icon/symbol):** A four-segment rounded-corner geometric shape resembling two overlapping squares or a flattened diamond with four colored quadrants. Each quadrant uses one of the four brand colors: **red-orange (#F24E1E) top-left, purple (#A259FF) top-right, blue-cyan (#1ABCFE) bottom-left, green (#0ACF83) bottom-right**. The overall silhouette is a rounded rectangle with an inner "figure-eight" or double-loop negative space in white. Visually, it reads as two interlocking/overlapping organic shapes. The mark is **multicolor by nature and cannot be simplified to a single color without losing identity.**
2. **The Wordmark:** "figma" in all-lowercase, set in what appears to be a **geometric humanist sans-serif, slightly custom** — close to Inter or a related face. The weight is regular to medium. The 'f' is distinctive. Letter-spacing is standard.

### Logo Characteristics

- **Typeface style:** Geometric humanist sans-serif, lowercase, very clean
- **Weight:** Regular/Medium
- **Tracking:** Normal, slightly tight
- **Case:** Always all-lowercase for the wordmark
- **Color:** The wordmark appears in **#000000 black** on light backgrounds throughout the marketing site
- **Mark color:** Always the four-color multicolor system

### Placement Rules

- The logo (mark + wordmark side-by-side) appears in the **upper-left corner** of the global navigation on all marketing pages, anchored left
- **Mark is left of wordmark**, mark and wordmark at equal height
- On the blog (Shortcut), the Figma mark appears as a small icon in the upper-right corner, with "SHORTCUT" being the primary logo for that sub-property
- On the Community platform, the Figma mark appears as a standalone icon in the upper left
- **Adequate whitespace** around the logo — never crowded by adjacent nav elements
- The logo links to the homepage universally

### Color Variations Observed

- **Full color mark + black wordmark:** Standard, used on white/light backgrounds (all product marketing pages)
- **Standalone mark:** Used in community platform, blog sub-brand context, favicon
- **Reverse/white:** Likely exists but not observed on the marketing site (which is exclusively light-background)
- **Black-only version:** Used in footer of the marketing site (mark appears to be rendered in black/dark there)

### Sizing Details

- **Global nav:** Mark approximately 28–32px tall
- **Favicon:** 16×16px simplified mark
- **Footer:** Approximately the same as nav size

### Brand Mark System

Figma has a **four-element brand mark system**:
1. **Full logo** (mark + wordmark): Primary identity
2. **Mark-only (icon):** Used in community, mobile, and app contexts
3. **Sub-brand wordmarks:** "FigJam," "Dev Mode," "Figma Sites," etc. are wordmark-only product names, always prefixed with "Figma" or standing alone
4. **Sub-property identity:** "Shortcut" (blog) has its own distinct wordmark system

### Logo Relationship to Brand Heritage/Positioning

The Figma mark's four colors and overlapping shape are a direct visual metaphor for **collaboration** — multiple entities overlapping and creating something at their intersection. The colors themselves reflect the full RGB spectrum (red, blue, green + purple), suggesting creative completeness. The all-lowercase wordmark suggests **approachability and democracy** — Figma is not hierarchical or corporate, it is for everyone. The mark's rounded, organic form contrasts with the precision of design tools, suggesting that creativity (not rigidity) is the product's core value.

### Product Card Anatomy (Community/Template Cards)

- **Image:** Full-bleed thumbnail (16:9 or 4:3 aspect ratio)
- **Title:** SemiBold, 14–16px, 1–2 lines
- **Author/Creator name:** Regular, small (12–13px), with linked profile
- **Category tags:** Small pill badges (e.g., "INSIDE FIGMA", "MAKER STORIES")
- **No pricing visible on community cards** (free resource library)
- **Hover behavior:** Cards lift slightly with a subtle shadow on hover (inferred from standard web pattern; not confirmed via JS inspection)

### Responsive Behavior

- **Desktop (1280px+):** Full 2-column feature layouts, 4-column pricing grid, 5-column footer
- **Tablet (~768–1279px):** 2-column pricing collapses to 2, feature sections may remain 2-column
- **Mobile (<768px):** All columns collapse to single-column stacks, nav collapses to hamburger menu, feature sections stack vertically (image above text)
- **Hero headings** reduce proportionally (large viewport: 80px → mobile: ~40px)

### Whitespace Strategy

**Whitespace is used as a primary design element.** Figma uses extremely generous section padding (80–120px vertical) between content blocks. This creates a sense of luxury and breathing room that signals premium positioning. The homepage, in particular, could fit 50% more content but deliberately leaves space — this is intentional brand restraint that mirrors the tool's own design philosophy (whitespace = visual hierarchy).

### Grid Alignment Philosophy

- Strict left-alignment for all text content (no centered body copy on product pages)
- Hero sections: Text is left-aligned within a ~50% width column
- Eyebrow labels are left-aligned
- The only centered content: Logo marquees, full-width testimonials, pricing page header, footer social icons
- This left-alignment creates a **reading gravity** that feels purposeful and editorial

### Homepage Scroll Architecture

1. Hero carousel (multi-slide, interactive)
2. Trusted logos (auto-scrolling marquee)
3. Feature block A: "Prompt, code, and design" + AI/Make feature
4. Testimonial pull-quote (Perplexity logo)
5. Feature block B: "Bring everyone together with systems that scale"
6. Feature block C: Sub-features (libraries, templates, Dev Mode)
7. Feature block D: "Ship products, any way you want" (Make, Sites, Code)
8. Testimonial (GitHub)
9. "Explore what people are making" gallery (community-made sites)
10. Template carousel (dark section)
11. Footer

**Total scroll depth:** ~8,000–12,000px on desktop. 11 distinct content modules.

### PDP Image/Gallery Layout

Product pages do not use traditional image galleries. Instead, they use **animated product screenshots** in a large browser chrome frame on the right side of the hero. As you scroll down, subsequent feature sections use **2-column illustration layouts** with the product UI shown in a card/device frame.

### Footer Layout

5-column footer on white background:
- **Column 1: PRODUCT** — 12 links
- **Column 2: PLANS** — 4 links
- **Column 3: USE CASES** — 16 links
- **Column 4: RESOURCES** — 20 links
- **Column 5: COMPANY** — 6 links
Above footer: Small logo + social icons row (X, YouTube, Instagram, Facebook)
Below columns: Language selector (English), legal links

### Layout Elements NOT Present

- **No sticky sidebar** on any page type
- **No mega menu with full-width takeover** (nav dropdowns are contained)
- **No sticky "back to top" button**
- **No chat widget** in the standard marketing experience (though this may be gated by user type)
- **No breadcrumbs** on any page
- **No comparison sliders** (before/after)
- **No pop-up/modal** on initial page load (other than cookie consent)
- **No floating CTA bar** (different from sticky nav CTAs)

## 8. Brand Voice & Language Style

### Tone: Overall Personality & Sub-categories

**Master Tone: Confident without arrogance. Enabling without patronizing. Smart without showing off.**

1. **Empowering voice:** Language that credits users with intelligence. "Turn big ideas into real products." Never "Figma makes it easy for even beginners."
2. **Action-oriented:** Almost every sentence uses an active verb. "Brainstorm, design, and build." "Create. Ship. Iterate." Verbs of making and doing.
3. **Collaborative framing:** The word "team" appears on virtually every page. "For teams who build products together." Figma is fundamentally about multiplayer work.
4. **Mission-driven clarity:** "Our vision is to make design accessible to all." Clear, unambiguous statement of purpose with democratic undertone.
5. **Understated technical precision:** When describing technical features, language is precise but not jargon-heavy. "version-controlled design file" — technically accurate, accessible to both designers and developers.
6. **Community pride:** Genuine celebration of what users build. "Explore what people are making." No corporate distance from the user community.
7. **Culture voice (Careers):** Warm, human, values-grounded. "We're multiplayer people who love the weird, wonderful magic that happens when people connect." Uses "magic" and "weird" — not typical corporate language.
8. **Self-aware playfulness:** "Nothing great is made alone." A simple, memorable belief statement. Not a tagline per se, but embedded in the Careers narrative.
9. **Educational voice (Help content/Blog):** Practical, example-driven. Blog section headers use active present-tense ("How we rebuilt," "Why we are so afraid").
10. **B2B credibility voice (Enterprise):** Shifts to business language — "Scale design. Reduce complexity." Fewer adjectives, more benefit statements. But still avoids generic enterprise platitudes.
11. **AI voice:** "Figma AI is your creative collaborator." Positions AI as assistant, not replacement — reassuring to designers who may fear AI disruption.
12. **Pricing voice:** Matter-of-fact and transparent. "Full seat $16/mo." No obfuscation, no "starting at," no asterisks in headlines.

### Language Patterns

**Founder/mission narrative:**
"Figma is a design platform for teams who build products together. Born on the Web, Figma helps teams create, share, and test designs for websites, mobile apps, and other digital products and experiences."
