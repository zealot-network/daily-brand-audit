## 1. Color Palette

### All Hex Colors Extracted From Site

Perplexity's visual language is built on a deliberately restrained, near-monochromatic palette with a single signature teal accent. Every color serves a structural role rather than a decorative one.

#### Primary Background Colors
- **#F5F0E8** — "Parchment / Warm Offwhite": The dominant background across all hub, blog, careers, help center, legal, pro, and 404 pages. A warm-toned, desaturated cream that reads as intelligent and editorial rather than clinical. Not a pure white; carries deliberate warmth.
- **#FAFAF8** — Near-white: Used in the main app interface (perplexity.ai search/home), the primary query pane, and modal backgrounds. Slightly cooler than the parchment.
- **#1A1A1A / #111111** — Near-black: Primary text color across the app interface, sidebars, and navigation on hub/marketing pages.

#### Teal Brand Color (Primary Accent)
- **#1B7A72** — Deep Teal: The Perplexity brand teal. Used as the primary CTA button background on hub/marketing pages ("Ask anything," "Ask Perplexity"), the "Get Started" CTA button on API Platform, and "Open Roles" button on careers. Also used as the active/selected state indicator beneath the "Top" tab in Discover.
- **#20808A** (approximate) — Medium Teal: Used in help center hero banner background wash. Appears on the privacy page header band. Also background for help center search bar submit button.
- **#2AA394** (approximate) — Medium-light Teal: Used for interactive pill filters on the hub blog topics section, hover states on pill buttons.
- **#0A9B8F** — Bright Teal: Observed in the API platform diagonal geometric hero graphic, as the dominant shape fill for the brand's tech/developer surface.

#### Text Colors
- **#1C2E2B** — Forest/Dark Teal-Black: Body copy and heading text on hub, blog, careers, legal pages. A near-black with distinct teal/green undertone—not a pure neutral. Conveys gravitas without being harsh.
- **#FFFFFF** — Pure White: CTA button text, navigation button text on dark surfaces.
- **#6B6B6B / #888888** — Medium Gray: Body text secondary level, metadata (dates, categories), caption text, footer fine print.
- **#B0A99A** — Warm Muted Tan: Used as ghost/placeholder text in the search input ("Ask anything…", "Type / for search modes").
- **#3D3D3D** — Dark Charcoal: Used in sidebar navigation text items (active state text in app).

#### UI Background Variants
- **#F0EBE0** — Slightly darker parchment: Card backgrounds on Pro page pricing cards.
- **#E5E0D5** — Border/separator color: Horizontal rules, card outlines, dividers between sections. Very subtle, low-contrast.
- **#E8E3D8** — Light parchment variant: Hover states on list items in the sidebar nav.
- **#2E2E2E / #252525** — Near-black surfaces: Dark hero images, hero backgrounds on Pro and API Platform. Creates dramatic contrast with product UI screenshots overlaid.
- **#0D1A17** — Deep forest black: Background color inside the hero imagery on Pro page—the AI model icon composite photograph.

#### Discover & Finance App Colors
- **#1B7A72** — Teal: Used as "Save Interests" button background in Discover panel.
- **#F2F2F2** — Light gray: Weather/finance widget cards.
- **#D4E8E5** — Light teal-mint: Subtle background tint for finance data cards and source attribution badges.
- **#EB5757** / Red tones: Stock ticker negative/down arrows (not a brand color—functional data visualization only).
- **#26A69A** / Green tones: Positive stock movement indicators (functional, not brand).

#### Comet Page Colors
- **#1A1A2E** — Deep navy/near-black: Comet hero section left sidebar sphere element.
- **#E63C32** — Coral Red: Comet hero abstract composition includes warm reds (photography/art direction, not interface).
- **#2E6B6E** — Teal-green: Comet hero circular abstract sphere, consistent with overall teal brand direction.

#### Key Observation on Palette Strategy

Perplexity's palette strategy is one of deliberate, almost architectural restraint. The parchment background (#F5F0E8) positions the brand as warm and intellectual rather than tech-clinical — it evokes the analog world of books, paper, and scholarship. The single teal accent (#1B7A72) does enormous brand work: it appears only where action is required (CTAs, active states, interactive elements), creating a powerful signal-to-noise ratio. There is zero decorative use of color. No gradients in the UI components. No rainbow palettes. The notable exception is photography and rendered hero images — where rich chromatic variety (the organic flower field for blog hero, the abstract colorful spheres for Comet) is deliberately contained within image frames, never leaking into the interface chrome. This creates a compositional strategy of "color as content" rather than "color as decoration."

#### Color Hierarchy & Emotional Role Analysis

1. **Teal (#1B7A72)** — Trust, Intelligence, Action. The brand's single "activation" color. Used only where the user should click or focus. Teal is scientifically associated with clarity of thought and trustworthiness — appropriate for an "answer engine."
2. **Parchment (#F5F0E8)** — Knowledge, Warmth, Gravitas. Positions Perplexity alongside books, libraries, academic institutions rather than silicon valley startups.
3. **Forest Dark (#1C2E2B)** — Authority, Depth. Used for all primary text — not a neutral black but a deliberate near-teal black that reinforces brand coherence even in typeset body copy.
4. **Near-White (#FAFAF8)** — Clarity, Focus. The app interface uses a cleaner, cooler near-white vs. the marketing pages' parchment — signaling a shift from "brand experience" to "tool experience."
5. **Mid-Gray (#888888)** — Supporting information, dates, metadata. Recedes deliberately.

#### Colors Deliberately Absent

- **Pure Red**: Never used as brand color. Only appears in stock data (functional).
- **Orange**: Not present anywhere in brand UI.
- **Purple/Violet**: Absent — Perplexity deliberately avoids the "AI purple" used by many competitors (Anthropic, etc.).
- **Blue**: Absent as a brand color. Teal is used instead of traditional tech blue (Perplexity vs. Microsoft, Google, OpenAI).
- **Yellow/Gold**: Not present in UI chrome (only appears in photographic elements).
- **Bright/Saturated Gradients**: No rainbow gradients on any interface surface.
- **Black (#000000 pure)**: Avoided — near-black tonal variants are used instead.
- **CSS drop shadows**: Not used on text or UI cards (only minimal box-shadow on modal layers).

## 2. Typography

### Font Families Identified

**Primary Display / Heading Font — "Galaxie Polaris" (or similar geometric condensed sans-serif)**
The careers page "CURIOSITY / WANTED" all-caps display headline uses a very wide, large geometric condensed typeface. The letterforms are clean, high-x-height, and architectural. Closest match: Founders Grotesk Condensed, or F Grotesk. Also observed in the giant "What's a 404?" heading on the 404 error page.

**Secondary Display / Editorial Serif — likely "Canela" or "Playfair Display" variant**
The Comet page uses a large, elegant serif for "The browser that works for you" — with high contrast stroke variation, bracketed serifs, and editorial proportions. The blog post headline "The AI is the Computer" uses a similar editorial serif. Closest match: Canela Text, Freight Display, or a custom version of a high-contrast serif. This typeface conveys intelligence, craft, and editorial weight.

**Interface / Body Sans-Serif — likely "DM Sans" or custom variant**
Used throughout the app interface (homepage query interface, sidebar navigation), body text on all hub/marketing pages, FAQ text, pricing information. Clean, geometric, slightly humanist sans-serif. Medium x-height, open counters, excellent legibility at small sizes. The Pro page heading "The most accurate answers from all the top AI models" also uses this font at large scale. Closest match: DM Sans, Inter, or a custom derivative.

**Monospace / Code Font — system monospace or "IBM Plex Mono"**
Changelog dates appear to use a monospaced or tabular-numeral style ("03.13.26", "03.06.26"). Blog post byline "Aravind Srinivas" and date "Mar 3, 2026" appears to use a distinct monospace-adjacent treatment — the author name is rendered in a tracked, spaced, technical-feeling style.

**Logo / Brand Wordmark — custom geometry**
The "perplexity" wordmark in the main app is a custom lowercase sans-serif with tight tracking. The Perplexity Pro variant appears to use the same wordmark family with "pro" in lighter weight italic/regular appended. Precise identification requires source file inspection but the letterforms suggest a bespoke or heavily modified version of a geometric sans.

### Typography Application By Page Type

**Homepage**: Clean, functional sans-serif throughout. Wordmark dominates visual hierarchy. Search placeholder text is the largest readable text. Suggestion button labels are 14–15px DM Sans. Category tabs ("Build a business") use small icon + 14px DM Sans medium.

**Pro Page**: Two distinct typographic registers — the editorial serif in data/imagery context is absent here; instead, the DM Sans expands to display scale (48–60px) for the hero headline. Sub-body is 16–17px. The pricing section introduces tabular numerals for prices. The FAQ section at bottom returns to accordion-style 16px body.

**Hub/Blog Index**: The featured article uses the editorial serif at display size (48px+). Article grid cards below use 16–18px DM Sans for titles. Topic filter pills: 14px DM Sans medium. Hero image spans full width with serif overlay text.

**Blog Post (Editorial)**: This is the most typographically rich context. Hero image full-width. Byline uses monospaced treatment. Body copy: 16–18px DM Sans, 1.7–1.8 line-height. H2s signal section breaks at 24–28px bold DM Sans. Pull text / opening paragraphs not distinctly styled. No drop caps, no decorative typographic elements.

**Careers**: Most dramatic typographic moment in the entire site. The "CURIOSITY WANTED" display type at 200–280px (viewport-filling) establishes an aspirational register that no other page matches. This is brand theater. Section labels like "MISSION," "VALUES," "BENEFITS" use ALL CAPS small DM Sans at 14px with wide tracking (~0.12em) — a classical editorial grid device. Job listings return to functional 14–16px.

**Legal/Privacy**: Light-weight DM Sans at 56px for "Perplexity Legal Hub." Body is 16px, accordion expanded text is 15–16px. No decorative typography.

**Help Center**: DM Sans for all text. The "How can we help?" H1 is 48–56px Regular weight. Supporting text is 18px. Search input placeholder is 15px. Very clean and minimal.

**API Platform**: DM Sans dominates. Large 48–60px headline. The dark hero creates the most dramatic contrast context.

**Comet**: The most typographically experimental sub-brand. Uses the editorial serif at very large scale (64–80px) alongside product-UI copy in DM Sans. The serif choice on Comet reinforces it as a premium, personal, human-focused product vs. the utilitarian search/API products.

### Font Pairing Philosophy

The pairing of editorial serif (Canela-type) with geometric humanist sans-serif (DM Sans-type) is a classically intelligent choice that encodes two complementary brand values:
- The **serif** communicates intellectual rigor, editorial authority, a connection to print culture, long-form knowledge, and human thought. It appears in brand identity moments (careers, Comet hero, major feature blog posts).
- The **sans-serif** communicates clarity, precision, utility, speed, and modern efficiency. It handles all functional interface copy.

Together they say: "We are a serious intellectual tool built by thoughtful, design-literate people." The pairing avoids the tech-company trap of going all-sans and appearing commoditized. It also avoids the editorial-brand trap of going all-serif and appearing slow or precious.

### Weight Distribution Analysis

- **Light (300)**: Large display headlines (Legal Hub, Help Center hero), suggesting elegance and restraint at big sizes.
- **Regular (400)**: Workhorse weight. All body copy, navigation items, blog text, sidebar labels.
- **Medium (500)**: Navigation CTAs, card headline labels, slightly emphasized UI elements.
- **SemiBold (600)**: Sub-section headers, footer column titles, Discover card headlines, careers values labels.
- **Bold (700)**: Section headers on blog posts, Values headings on careers, Pricing plan names.
- **Heavy/Black (900)**: Careers hero "CURIOSITY WANTED" only. This is the maximum weight moment on the entire site — used for exactly one purpose.

### Letter-Spacing Rules

- All-caps labels: +0.08–0.15em (careers section labels, footer column headers)
- Display headlines (DM Sans at 48px+): -0.01 to -0.02em (optical tracking reduction)
- Body copy: 0em (no adjustment)
- Blog byline / metadata: +0.05–0.08em (monospace treatment)
- CTA buttons: 0 to +0.02em

## 3. Logo & Wordmark

### Primary Logo Description

Perplexity operates three distinct logo systems across its sub-brand properties:

**1. Main App / Hub Logo (perplexity.ai app, hub, blog, help center, careers, legal)**
A combination mark: a snowflake-like geometric symbol (the Perplexity "prism" or "asterisk" logomark) paired with the wordmark "perplexity" in lowercase custom sans-serif. On hub/marketing pages, the full combination mark appears in dark forest-teal (#1C2E2B) at approximately 32–40px height in the top-left. The symbol appears at approximately 30×30px to the left of the wordmark.

**2. Perplexity Pro Logo (perplexity.ai/pro)**
A compact horizontal combination: lowercase "perplexity" wordmark followed by "pro" in a lighter, potentially italic variant. Rendered in dark charcoal/near-black on the parchment background. Sits top-left at ~36px height.

**3. Perplexity Enterprise Logo (perplexity.ai/enterprise)**
Text-based horizontal lockup: "perplexity" wordmark + "enterprise" suffix, rendered in dark on parchment. Appears with slightly more formal treatment.

**4. Perplexity API Platform Logo**
"perplexity | API Platform" — uses a vertical separator and "API Platform" in regular weight alongside the wordmark.

**5. Comet Logo (perplexity.ai/comet)**
A distinct sub-brand treatment: stylized "comet" wordmark in a different typeface (appears rounded, lowercase, slightly playful) with the Perplexity geometric mark adjacent.

### Logo Characteristics

**Wordmark typeface style**: Custom geometric lowercase sans-serif. Very clean, medium x-height, slightly rounded terminal forms. Not a standard retail typeface — appears custom-drawn or significantly modified from a base like Söhne, Graphik, or Matter. Key identifying features: the lowercase "p" has an open counter; the "x" has a clean intersection; the "y" descender is moderate length.

**Weight**: Regular to Medium (400–500). Never bold in the wordmark itself.

**Tracking**: Tight — approximately -0.03 to -0.05em letter-spacing on the wordmark. This creates a confident, compact brand presence.

**The Geometric Symbol**: A six-armed, symmetrical snowflake/asterisk/prism shape. The arms appear to be constructed from diamond/rhombus forms, creating a faceted, crystalline appearance. References perplexity = complexity = many facets of knowledge. Color: same dark forest-teal as the text, or white on dark surfaces.

### Placement Rules

- Always positioned top-left on all hub, blog, legal, careers pages
- On the app interface (homepage), the wordmark appears centered above the search bar — the singular centering moment for the mark
- No tagline accompanies the mark in any context observed
- Adequate clear space of approximately 1× the logomark height on all sides
- On dark backgrounds (API Platform), the mark appears in white
- On parchment/light backgrounds, the mark appears in dark forest-teal

### Color Variations Observed

1. **Dark (forest teal #1C2E2B)** on parchment — primary usage
2. **White (#FFFFFF)** on dark backgrounds (API Platform header)
3. **Dark on parchment with sub-brand text** (Pro, Enterprise)

### Sizing

- Navigation header: approximately 32–40px tall (full lockup)
- Standalone mark on careers sub-nav: approximately 28–32px
- App homepage centered wordmark: approximately 36–48px

### Brand Mark System

Perplexity uses a clear dual-system:
1. **Full lockup (symbol + wordmark)**: Used on hub pages, marketing, blog header
2. **Symbol alone**: Used as favicon, app icon, within UI (sidebar icon for Search function)
3. **Wordmark alone**: Used in some contexts for maximum legibility at small sizes

Sub-brands (Pro, Enterprise, Comet, API Platform) follow a consistent pattern of "[primary mark] + [sub-brand descriptor]" either in a horizontal lockup or stacked.

### Logo Relationship to Brand Heritage/Positioning

The geometric crystalline mark is a sophisticated encoding of the brand's core proposition: Perplexity means "state of bewilderment" — the first moment of a new question. The snowflake/prism geometry suggests multi-faceted intelligence, many paths of inquiry, the refraction of information into clear answers. It is structurally reminiscent of OpenAI's mark but distinct in its organic, faceted quality vs. OpenAI's more mechanical gear/atom form. The lowercase wordmark is a studied choice: lowercase communicates approachability, modernity, and accessibility — Perplexity as a collaborative thinking partner, not a corporate authority.

## 4. Layout & Grid System

### Global Structure

**Marketing/Hub Pages (blog, careers, legal, help center, API platform, pro):**
- Max content width: approximately 1200–1280px
- Body margins: 40–60px on desktop (24–32px on mobile)
- Header height: approximately 64px on hub pages; sticky on careers sub-nav
- Footer: 6-column grid (Company, Product, Resources, API Platform, Follow Us, App Store badges) at ~1200px max-width

**App Interface (perplexity.ai homepage):**
- Left sidebar: fixed, ~185px wide
- Content area: remaining viewport, centered search box
- Search box: ~580–600px max-width, horizontally centered
- The interface uses a unique two-panel layout with sidebar + main content

**Padding / Gaps:**
- Section padding: 80–120px top/bottom on marketing pages
- Card grids: 24–32px gap
- Content internal padding: 40–48px horizontal

### Page Templates

**Homepage (app interface):**
Left sidebar navigation (fixed, dark-ish) + main content area (clean near-white). Single-focus layout: one centered search input with contextual suggestion cards below. Zero visual clutter. The sidebar contains: logo, Search, Computer, New thread, History, Discover, Spaces, Finance, More, Recent threads, Sign In. The main area contains exclusively the wordmark + input + category tabs + suggestion prompts.

**Pro Page:**
Split hero (50% left text / 50% right image). Below: alternating full-width feature sections. Pricing section: centered, 3-column card grid. FAQ at bottom: single-column accordion. No mega-menu; single top bar with "Ask Perplexity" CTA only.

**Hub/Blog Index:**
Fixed top nav (32px height approx) + full-width hero (one featured article, image left, text right) + Topics filter pills row + search input + 3-column article card grid below.

**Blog Post (Editorial):**
Top nav + full-width hero image (approx 80vh) + byline row + centered single-column body copy (max-width 680–720px) + footer. Very classic long-form editorial layout. No sidebars, no related articles sidebar. Pure reading focus.

**Careers:**
Unique sub-navigation bar (sticky, centered pill shape with nav links). Full-viewport hero (text-only, giant display type). Then scrolling sections: Mission, Values (2-column text), Benefits (tabbed: US/International), Locations (map or text list), Hiring Process (numbered steps), Open Roles (accordion by department). Footer at bottom.

**Legal/Privacy:**
Hero band with teal background (unique across the site — the only page with a teal header band). Below: two-column layout — left sidebar (accordion categories), right main content. Classic legal document design.

**Help Center:**
Teal hero with search (same teal as legal header). Below fold: parchment, with content blocks for FAQs, getting started, etc.

**API Platform:**
Dark-background hero (unique dark theme). Below: light section for API products. Then case studies, resources, CTA sections.

**Comet:**
Unique design system. Parchment background but with abstract photography as ambient elements (colored spheres, geometric shapes bleeding off screen edges). Centered single-column hero text. Product UI mockup below.

**404 Error Page:**
Full-viewport centered layout. Giant "What's a 404?" display text (200px+). Simple paragraph + single CTA button. Footer is fully rendered (reveals full sitemap).

### Product Card Anatomy (Blog Article Cards)

Article cards on hub/blog index:
- Full-width image (16:9 ratio approximately)
- Date + Category tags below image (small text, warm gray)
- Headline text (16–18px, DM Sans medium, dark)
- No explicit CTA button — entire card is clickable
- No hover animation observed (image doesn't zoom/scale)
- No author byline on index cards

### Responsive Behavior

- At mobile breakpoints (<768px), the sidebar nav collapses entirely; the main app interface becomes single-column
- Hub page 3-column article grid shifts to 1-column on mobile
- The hub hero splits to single-column (image above text)
- Pro page hero splits to stacked single-column
- Careers "CURIOSITY WANTED" scales proportionally but remains full-viewport
- Footer 6-column grid collapses to 2-column on tablet, 1-column on mobile

### Whitespace Strategy

Perplexity uses whitespace as a primary design element. The parchment background functions as generous "breathing room" — sections have very substantial padding (80–120px). This is not the dense information-packing of a typical SaaS tool; it's magazine editorial cadence applied to a software product. The search homepage is the most extreme example: a nearly empty page with one centered input. This communicates confidence — "we don't need to explain ourselves; just ask."

### Grid Alignment Philosophy

Everything snaps to a consistent left-margin. The multi-column sections (careers values, pricing cards) use CSS grid/flexbox with even gaps. No intentional asymmetry in the layout grid itself. The dramatic asymmetry comes from photography/imagery treatment — full-bleed images next to contained text — rather than from the underlying grid math.

## 8. Brand Voice & Language Style

### Tone: Overall Personality

Perplexity's brand voice is that of a **brilliant, confident intellectual who respects your intelligence**. Not a corporate entity trying to seem approachable, but a genuine intellectual peer — precise, direct, occasionally grandiose in vision, but always substantiated.

10 sub-category analysis:

1. **Visionary Declarative**: Makes large claims without hedging. "The most accurate answers from all the top AI models." "The AI is the Computer." "Power your deep work." Speaks in present tense as if the future is already here.

2. **Philosophical Confidence**: The blog post "The AI is the Computer" by Aravind Srinivas is written like a founder manifesto crossed with a philosophy paper. References Steve Jobs, compares AI model makers to Antonio Stradivari. High cultural literacy assumed.

3. **Technical Precision with Accessibility**: Engineering blog posts discuss "Disaggregated Prefill and Decode" — deeply technical — but all product copy strips jargon. Never talks down; never over-explains.

4. **Intellectual Respect**: The careers page: "Perplexity is AI for people who expect more. More accuracy. More agency. More signal, less noise." Assumes the reader is smart. No "easy" or "simple" language.

5. **Understated Utility**: Marketing copy rarely uses superlatives beyond the core claims. "Get comprehensive, cited reports in minutes." Direct statement, no adjectival inflation.

6. **Scholarly Authority**: Positioning ("Trust every answer," "Every answer includes inline citations") borrows from academic/journalistic standards. Perplexity frames itself as operating at the standards of serious research, not Google search.

7. **Community Building Through Mission**: Careers framing ("Join us to craft tools for great minds") and the values (Craftsmanship, Ownership, Entrepreneurship, Scholarship, Partnership) create a vocabulary of intellectual belonging. The word "Scholarship" as a company value is almost unheard of in tech.

8. **Self-Aware Wit**: "What's a 404?" — treats an error page as an opportunity for brand voice. Not "Page Not Found" but a rhetorical question. The Comet tagline "Browse at the speed of thought" is a philosophical provocation.

9. **Strategic Combativeness**: "Bullying is Not Innovation" (blog post title) suggests the brand is willing to engage in public positioning against competitors. This is unusual restraint — only one such post observed in hundreds.

10. **Warm Directness**: Customer-facing copy ("How can we help?" / "Get answers to all things Perplexity") is warm without being saccharine. Not exclamation-point enthusiasm; genuine helpfulness.

### Language Patterns

**Founder/Vision Narrative**: "We recently launched Perplexity Computer. This post explains our thinking behind it." — Transparent, first-person, explains the "why" behind products. Assumes the reader cares about reasoning, not just features.

**Product Description Structure**: Name → single-line purpose → key differentiators. "Perplexity Computer. Nineteen models are orchestrated for you." Minimal. Let the product speak.

**Heading Capitalization**: Title Case for most H2/H3 headings on marketing pages. Sentence case for body copy. ALL CAPS for careers section labels ("MISSION," "VALUES," "BENEFITS") — a deliberate shift in register, signaling institutional/foundational importance.

**Collection/Section Landing Pattern**: Each section opens with a bold aspirational statement, then narrows to specifics. Careers: "Perplexity is AI for people who expect more." → "More accuracy. More agency." → specific benefit list. A funnel from vision to proof.

**Material/Technical Specification Language**: Used in API and blog posts. "Low-latency hybrid search, combining semantic methods, LLM ranking, and human feedback." Precise technical vocabulary without apology or simplification.

**Geographic/Cultural Specificity**: Careers page names all office locations specifically (San Francisco, Palo Alto, New York, Austin, Washington D.C., London, Berlin, Belgrade). Perplexity doesn't say "global" abstractly — it names the places.

**Price Presentation**: "$17/month when billed annually" — clear and specific. "Starting from free" pattern with named tier upgrades. No "pricing starts at" obfuscation.

**Partnership Language**: Announcement posts use the pattern "Perplexity + [Partner Name]" (Perplexity x Cristiano Ronaldo, Perplexity x Lewis Hamilton). Casual, equal partnership framing — no "proud to announce" corporate formality.

**Copyright/Legal footer**: "© Copyright 2026 Perplexity" — minimal. No "All rights reserved" padding.
