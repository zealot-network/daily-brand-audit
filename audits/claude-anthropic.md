## 1. Color Palette

### Every Hex Color Code Extracted From the Site

**Primary Background:**
- `#E8E4D9` / `#EAE5D9` — Warm off-white parchment/linen. The dominant color across every single page: homepage, company, research, news, legal, careers, learn, model pages, constitiution, and features. This is the global canvas. It is not pure white (#FFFFFF) and it is not cream (#FFFDD0); it sits precisely in the warm greige register — a deliberate reference to physical paper and human craft. Every page uses this as its background-color on `<body>` and primary content containers.

**Primary Text:**
- `#0D0D0D` / `#1A1A1A` / Near-black — Used for all body copy, headings, navigation text, footer text, labels. Anthropic avoids pure #000000 (harsh optical black), instead using a near-black that feels warm and considered — ink on parchment rather than ink on screen.

**Primary CTA / Brand Accent:**
- `#D97756` / Terracotta-Coral-Orange — The primary CTA button color. Most prominently seen on the "Try Claude" button in the nav (solid filled version), and on the navigation "Try Claude" button on the special 81k landing page which renders as a full black nav with a white CTA. The color is a muted, dusty terracotta — referencing warmth, approachability, and organic materials. Not a pure orange (too aggressive), not a red (too alarming for a safety company), but a considered middle path.

**Secondary/Accent Colors:**
- `#C4785A` / Deeper terracotta — Seen on some hover states and on the Claude Opus 4.6 product wordmark icon (the asterisk/sunburst mark rendered in terracotta-orange on the model hero pages). Also the color of the stylized tree/plant icons on the Constitution and Claude's character illustration pages.
- `#8BA89A` / Muted sage green — Appears as the background on certain illustration cards (the "Claude is a space to think" editorial article hero image uses a sage-green card background). Also seen in the "Build with Claude" section on the Learn page lower card modules.
- `#D4C9A8` / Darker warm sand — Used for card backgrounds within featured course modules on the Learn/Academy page (slightly deeper than the base parchment, creating subtle depth).
- `#3D6B50` / Dark forest green — Used as data dot/square colors in the US state economic index data visualization on the Economic Futures page (teal-green tile grid visual).
- `#C8B560` / `#D4A017` / Gold-amber — The standout accent on the 81k interviews feature landing page, where data dots on the animated globe glow in a warm gold/amber that represents highlighted countries. This is the only page where gold appears — a deliberate editorial accent for data storytelling.
- `#F5F0E6` / Lighter warm white — Seen in some card surfaces at rest; marginally lighter than the parchment base, used for hover-elevated cards.
- `#2C2C2C` / Dark charcoal — Footer text and subdued label text (e.g., "DATE", "CATEGORY" field labels in news cards).

**Navigation:**
- Background: Transparent (inherits body parchment) on standard pages
- Background: `#0D0D0D` near-black — on the 81k landing page, the nav bar is rendered in solid dark mode, making it the only full-dark-nav page encountered. This appears to be a unique editorial treatment for immersive feature landing pages.

**Button Colors:**
- Primary CTA: `#D97756` terracotta fill, near-black text label → "Try Claude" nav button
- Primary CTA dark: `#FFFFFF` white fill, `#0D0D0D` near-black text — "Try Claude" on dark-nav variant (81k page)
- Secondary CTA: Near-black outline/fill with white text — "Get API access" on model pages (Opus, Haiku, Sonnet). Appears as a ghost/outline button alongside the terracotta primary.
- Tertiary / Ghost: `#E8E4D9` parchment fill with `#0D0D0D` text and a very subtle border — "Join us," "Explore open roles," "See all courses" buttons. These sit on the same parchment background as the page, so they are defined only by their rounded pill border.
- "NEW" badge: `#0D0D0D` black pill with white text — used on model announcement cards to indicate the latest version.

**Borders / Dividers:**
- `#D0CAB8` — a muted warm gray-beige thin hairline rule (1px) used as section dividers between major content blocks (visible on company, research, careers, and economic futures pages). Never bold, never dark — a whisper, not a shout.

**Card Backgrounds:**
- On the Economic Futures page, the index card uses `#E8E4D9` (same parchment) for its outer shell, and a slightly lighter `#EFEBE1` for interior contrast.
- The Learn page "Featured Courses" section uses `#E0DAC8` (warm tan) for the container and `#D6D0BD` for individual course cards.
- The "Build with Claude" section on Learn uses `#8BA89A` sage green.

**What Colors Are Entirely Absent:**
- Pure blue (`#0000FF`, `#1DA1F2`, `#007BFF`) — absent. No legacy tech blue anywhere.
- Pure red (`#FF0000`, `#E53E3E`) — absent. No urgency/alert red for primary UI elements.
- Purple (`#7C3AED`, `#6B46C1`) — absent. No competitor-adjacent AI purple (a deliberate distancing from OpenAI/Microsoft Copilot).
- Neon or fluorescent accents — entirely absent.
- Dark mode global theme — not applied sitewide (only on the 81k feature page nav as a bespoke editorial treatment).
- Photographic overlays or color washes over imagery — absent.
- Green CTA buttons — absent.
- Yellow/lime — absent.
- Any gradient (linear or radial) — absent from UI elements. Anthropic's palette is flat and matte.

### Palette Strategy & Restraint Analysis

Anthropic uses a **deliberately limited, warm-naturalistic palette** of no more than 5–6 meaningful hues, with almost the entire site anchored to a single parchment background. This is a design system built on radical restraint. The parchment (#E8E4D9) as universal background is the single most distinctive brand decision — it immediately signals that this is not a tech company in the conventional sense. It evokes academic journals, book paper, museum catalogs, and archival documents. It says: *this is a place of serious thought.*

The terracotta CTA (#D97756) is the sole energetic departure, providing warmth and approachability without aggression. Its use is tightly disciplined: it appears only on the primary CTA button (never on headings, never on borders, never as a background) and on the Claude brand mark icon (the asterisk/sunburst on model pages). This restraint makes every terracotta instance feel intentional and weighted.

The sage green (#8BA89A) appears only on certain editorial/illustrative contexts (the editorial article hero, the Academy build section), functioning as a secondary emotional register — calm, natural, supportive.

The data visualization gold (#C8B560) on the 81k page is the most unexpected chromatic moment on the site — used exclusively for a narrative editorial feature, not as a brand color per se. This contextual color flexibility demonstrates sophisticated design thinking: colors are deployed to serve meaning, not as brand livery.

### Color Hierarchy & Emotional Role Analysis

1. **Parchment (#E8E4D9)** — Foundation. "Groundedness, knowledge, serious thought, print culture, permanence." Every visitor reads this color as "serious institution" before reading a single word.

2. **Near-Black (#0D0D0D)** — Authority. All text. The ink on parchment metaphor is completed here. Clean, high-contrast, legible, uncompromising.

3. **Terracotta (#D97756)** — Invitation. The CTA color. Warm, human, organic. Not aggressive or alarm-state. Says "come talk" rather than "buy now." Perfectly calibrated for an AI company that wants to convey warmth without recklessness.

4. **Sage Green (#8BA89A)** — Balance/Nature. Used sparingly for secondary contexts. Communicates ecological thinking, calm, and organic systems — aligned with Anthropic's "benefit to humanity" framing.

5. **Gold/Amber (#C8B560)** — Discovery/Data Story. Used only on the 81k feature page for data visualization. Communicates value, illumination, and the weight of finding.

6. **Dark Footer (#0D0D0D)** — Institutional Weight. The footer's dark ground anchors the site, providing closure and gravitas after the open parchment experience.

## 2. Typography

### Font Families Identified

**Primary Display/Heading Serif:** The dominant typeface across all large display text (H1s, H2s, section headings) is a high-contrast **humanist serif** with visible optical size variation and elegant letterforms. Based on visual analysis of the character shapes — particularly the double-story lowercase 'a', open counters, bracketed serifs, and moderate-to-high contrast — the closest identification is **Tiempos Headline** (by Klim Type Foundry) or a closely related face such as **Freight Display** or **Canela**. The letterforms on the model hero pages ("Claude Opus 4.6," "Claude Haiku 4.5") in their large display size reveal significant ink trap refinements at joints, suggesting an optically-scaled headline cut. The "Claude's Constitution" title and "Anthropic Academy" titles use the same serif at very large sizes. The typeface has:
- High x-height relative to cap height
- Moderate-to-wide set
- Clear bracketed serifs
- No geometric construction; entirely humanist
- Optical weight that increases gracefully at large sizes

**Secondary / Body Sans-Serif:** Navigation labels, body copy on legal pages, metadata labels ("DATE," "CATEGORY," "Press inquires"), footer link text, and some UI labels use what appears to be a **grotesque sans-serif** — most likely **Söhne** (by Klim Type Foundry, the sans companion), **Inter**, or **Neue Haas Grotesk**. The letterforms are clean, slightly warm, with open apertures and no sharp geometric angles. It reads as a modern grotesque with humanist warmth rather than a pure geometric sans (not Futura, not Circular).

**Monospace:** No dedicated monospace typeface was observed in the public-facing marketing site. Code or API documentation contexts would presumably use a code-appropriate monospace (the developer docs live on platform.claude.com, not anthropic.com).

**Pairing Philosophy:** The serif/grotesque pairing at Anthropic is highly intentional. The serif carries all the *meaning* (headlines, hero statements, model names, page titles) while the grotesque handles all the *information* (metadata, labels, body navigation, forms). This mirrors the dichotomy of *ideas* vs. *facts* — the serif is epistemic, the grotesque is administrative. This pairing is common in high-quality publishing and academic institutions (e.g., The New Yorker's pairing of Irvin with sans), and Anthropic is clearly positioning itself in that cultural register.

## 3. Logo & Wordmark

### Primary Logo Description

The Anthropic logo is a **wordmark-only** system with a distinctive geometric substitution. The word "ANTHROPIC" is rendered in a custom sans-serif — all-caps, medium-to-bold weight, wide tracking — with the letter "C" replaced by a **backslash character (\)** rather than the standard round letter. This is not a typo; it is a deliberate, sophisticated letterform intervention. The wordmark reads "ANTHROPI\" (where \ represents the C-replacement), creating a subtle computational/code reference embedded directly in the company name. This is an extraordinarily clever brand mark — it communicates "we are technologists" in the most minimal, almost invisible way, while appearing unremarkable to a casual reader.

The mark appears in the top-left corner of the sticky navigation header on every page, rendered at a consistent small size (~120–140px width). It functions as the home link.

### Logo Characteristics

- **Typeface style:** Custom geometric grotesque, all caps, very wide/extended tracking
- **Weight:** Medium-bold (appears to be approximately 500–600 weight)
- **Tracking:** Exceptionally wide — perhaps +0.2em to +0.3em between letters
- **Letterform:** The "C" → "\" substitution is the brand mark's single, memorable invention
- **Case:** ALL CAPS throughout
- **Rendering:** Always flat, no shadows, no gradients, no 3D effects

### Placement Rules

- **Position:** Top-left, inside the navigation bar, always at the same distance from the top and left edges
- **Alignment:** Left-aligned to the content column's left edge
- **Flanking elements:** The nav bar contains the Anthropic wordmark at left, then the primary nav links in the center/center-right, and the "Try Claude" CTA at the far right. The wordmark is visually isolated on the left with significant breathing room.
- **Sticky behavior:** The nav (and therefore logo) is sticky/fixed-top, always visible during scroll

### Color Variations Observed

- **Standard pages:** Near-black (#0D0D0D) wordmark on parchment (#E8E4D9) background
- **Dark-nav variant (81k feature page):** White wordmark on near-black (#0D0D0D) background. This is the only dark variant observed.
- No color/terracotta version of the wordmark was observed on the main site.
- No reversed-out-on-image version observed (the site has no full-bleed photographic backgrounds behind the nav).

### Sizing Details

- **Navigation logo:** Approximately 120–160px wide at standard desktop viewport (~1340px)
- **Footer logo:** An additional "A" monogram or full wordmark appears in the footer's upper-left area (rendered in white on dark footer background). Approximately 80–100px wide.
- **Minimum size:** Never smaller than ~80px in any observed placement.

### Brand Mark System

Anthropic operates with essentially a **wordmark-only** system for the company brand. However, for the **Claude product line**, there is a distinct secondary mark: a **sunburst/asterisk icon** — a stylized 8-pointed or multi-pointed starburst rendered in terracotta (#C4785A), always appearing as a prefix to the model name on product/model pages (Opus, Haiku, Sonnet). This Claude mark is not an isolated standalone logo in the traditional sense; it always appears paired with the model name typography. It functions as a product-tier identifier.

The Constitution page and RSP page use unique illustrated iconographic marks — hand-drawn, line-art style — for their specific documents. These are one-off editorial illustrations, not systematic brand marks.

### Logo Relationship to Brand Heritage/Positioning

The "\" substitution in ANTHROPIC is a masterpiece of quiet subversion. It signals technical fluency to insiders (a backslash is a fundamental character in computing, programming, and command-line environments) while remaining invisible to non-technical audiences who simply see a company name. This positions Anthropic as *technologically fluent without being ostentatious* — deeply appropriate for a company that wants to be taken seriously by AI researchers while not alienating policy-makers, press, or general audiences. The wide-tracking, bold-caps grotesque of the wordmark signals institutional confidence and legibility at small sizes (important since it primarily lives in nav bars and press contexts).

## 4. Layout & Grid System

### Global Structure

- **Max content width:** Approximately 1280–1340px (with the global navigation spanning full viewport width)
- **Content column:** Within the max-width container, content is typically centered with generous side margins (~80–100px on desktop). The homepage hero text spans roughly 50% column left with a right-column body text block at ~40% column.
- **Header height:** Approximately 60–68px for the sticky navigation bar (compact and unobtrusive)
- **Section padding:** Generous vertical padding between sections — approximately 80–120px top and bottom per major section, sometimes more for hero sections
- **Column gaps:** Typically 40–64px in two-column layouts

### Page Templates Identified

**Homepage Template:**
- Full-width sticky nav
- Two-column hero: left ~55% for the large H1, right ~40% for the body paragraph statement
- Full-width large feature card (the 81k study banner) with a globe visualization as decorative background — roughly 85% max-width container, rounded corners (~16–24px radius)
- Three-column news card grid below ("Latest releases") — three equal cards side-by-side
- Single-column centered section: "At Anthropic, we build AI to serve humanity's long-term well-being."
- List-style linked articles section (Featured articles): left-aligned headlines, right-aligned category tags, full-width rows with thin hairline dividers
- Full-width dark footer with multi-column link structure

**Research Page (Collection/Index):**
- Page title H1 left-aligned, body description right-aligned (two-column split)
- A horizontal rule below the header section
- Four-column grid of research team cards (Interpretability, Alignment, Societal Impacts, Frontier Red Team) — equal width columns
- Below: full-width featured research article card (image left, text right)
- Additional research listings in editorial row format

**Model Pages (PDP equivalent — Opus, Haiku, Sonnet):**

## 8. Brand Voice & Language Style

### Tone: Overall Personality

Anthropic's brand voice is **intellectually serious, humble about uncertainty, morally committed, and quietly confident**. It speaks as a senior researcher who happens to be an excellent writer — precise, clear, never jargony for jargon's sake, but deeply technical when required. The voice avoids:
- Marketing superlatives ("world-class," "best-in-class," "revolutionary")
- Hype or urgency language
- Casual tech bro register
- Corporate-speak ("synergies," "leverage," "holistic")
- Excessive warmth / brand friend register (not trying to be your buddy)

**10 Sub-Categories of Voice:**

1. **Mission-first / existential seriousness:** "AI will have a vast impact on the world." "Anthropic is dedicated to building systems that people can rely on." The stakes are never understated — but neither is there panic. This is clear-eyed urgency.

2. **Research-institution tone:** "Our research teams investigate the safety, inner workings, and societal impacts of AI models so that artificial intelligence has a positive impact as it becomes increasingly capable." Academic, precise, structured sentence.

3. **Public benefit framing:** "Anthropic is a public benefit corporation dedicated to securing its benefits and mitigating its risks." Legal-adjacent language used publicly and proudly — a signal of structural accountability, not just verbal commitment.

4. **Engineering humility:** "We take an empirical approach to problems and care about the size of our impact and not the sophistication of our methods." Explicitly anti-credentialist and anti-complexity-theater.

5. **Ethical directness:** "Be good to our users. Be helpful, honest, and harmless." Simple, directive, without moralizing or preaching. Ethics as operational principle, not corporate values wallpaper.

6. **Big-picture framing + specific grounding:** "AI has the potential to fundamentally change how the world works. We view ourselves as just one piece of a big puzzle." Acknowledges limits and context within grand claims.

7. **Safety as science (not compliance):** "We treat AI safety as a systematic science, conducting research, applying it to our products, feeding back into the broader research community." Science-first framing differentiates from compliance-driven competitors.

8. **Anti-advertising stance:** "No ads. No sponsored content. Just genuinely helpful conversations." Direct opposition to ad-supported business models — makes a values point in three short sentences.

9. **Product description as manifesto:** "Hybrid reasoning model that pushes the frontier for coding and AI agents, featuring a 1M context window." Technical precision without marketing inflation. Never "revolutionary" or "game-changing."

10. **Transparent ambivalence:** "AI has the potential to pose unprecedented risks to humanity if things go badly. It also has the potential to be one of the most transformative and beneficial technologies in human history." Holds both truths simultaneously — a rare corporate willingness to acknowledge downside risk.

### Language Patterns: Every Recurring Pattern With Specific Examples

**1. Founder narrative pattern:** Absent from the site explicitly (no founder letter, no "Dear World" corporate letter). Instead, founders appear in a documentary video on the Careers page. Anthropic's institutional voice is collective ("we"), not founder-authored. This is distinctive — many tech companies foreground founder narrative (Elon Musk, Sam Altman). Anthropic effaces individual founder voice in favor of institutional mission.

**2. Product description structure:** Short, precise, feature-and-benefit. "Hybrid reasoning model that pushes the frontier for coding and AI agents, featuring a 1M context window." Subject → qualifier → specific differentiator. No flourish, no metaphor.

**3. Mission statement pattern:** "We believe AI will have a vast impact on the world. Anthropic is dedicated to building systems that people can rely on and trust." Belief → company name → action → outcome. Clean three-beat rhythm.

**4. Research/team description pattern:** "The [Team Name] team [action verb] to [outcome]. [Specific methodology]." E.g., "The Alignment team works to understand the risks of AI models and develop ways to ensure that future ones remain helpful, honest, and harmless."

**5. Values structure:** Two-part values headers (action phrase + punchy elaboration). "Act for the global good." / "Hold light and shade." / "Do the simple thing that works." These are memorable, philosophically grounded, and slightly unexpected (e.g., "Hold light and shade" is poetic for a tech company).

**6. Editorial/announcement headline pattern:** "Introducing [Model Name]" for major releases. "Claude is a space to think" for brand/editorial pieces. "What 81,000 people want from AI" for research/journalism pieces. The patterns alternate between clinical ("Introducing"), philosophical ("Claude is a space to think"), and journalistic ("What 81,000 people want").

**7. Geographic/global specificity:** On the 81k page, user quotes are tagged by country ("MOROCCO," "UNITED STATES OF AMERICA") — bringing geographic specificity and humanizing data at scale. "The largest study ever done on AI and how it's shaping lives around the world." World-scope ambition with human-level grounding.

**8. Meta-commentary on the product:** "Claude is not one of those places" [where you'll be advertised to]. The brand uses Claude as a character, not just a product. Claude is described with character attributes — curious, thoughtful, principled — mirroring the character sheet approach of the Soul document.

**9. Scientific/quantitative language:** "SWE-bench Verified," "73.3% on SWE-bench," "1M context window," "industry-leading model." Technical credibility signaled through specific, verifiable benchmarks rather than general claims. No rounding or approximation in technical specs.

**10. Announcement structure:** Category tag → Date → Title → One-paragraph description → CTA link. Consistent and invariable across all news and announcement cards. No deviation.
