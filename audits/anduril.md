## 1. Color Palette

### Every Hex Color Extracted from the Site

**Primary Background Colors:**
- `#000000` — Pure black: the dominant background across all product pages (Ghost, Roadrunner, Fury, Dive-XL, Space, Arsenal-1, Careers, Open Roles, Homepage hero). This is the brand's alpha and omega — the color of the night sky, stealth technology, and classified secrecy.
- `#FFFFFF` — Pure white: used as the hero background on the editorial manifesto page (Rebooting the Arsenal of Democracy), article pages (news detail), and the Mission/About page hero. White signals authority, transparency, academic rigor.
- `#E8E6DF` / `#DDDBD4` — Warm sage-off-white (approximately): the Lattice product suite pages and News index use this muted, desaturated sage-grey-green tone. Approximate hex range `#D6D5CC`–`#E4E2DA`. It reads as institutional parchment — connoting intelligence, software, data, networks.
- `#1A1A0A` — Near-black with a very subtle olive/brown warmth: the actual "black" used in footer backgrounds and the loading state of the homepage hero. Not pure black — has a micro-warm olive undertone visible in the olive-tinged darkness of the manufacturing facility shots.

**Text Colors:**
- `#FFFFFF` — White: primary text on all black-background pages (nav links, product name wordmarks, subheadings, category labels, bracket codes, product descriptors).
- `#000000` / `#0D0D0D` — Near-black: primary body text on white and sage backgrounds (Mission page, editorial pages, news articles, Lattice Mesh page).
- `#3D3D35` / `#4A4A44` — Dark charcoal/olive-grey: used for secondary body text and microcopy on light-background pages. On the Lattice Mesh page, the body copy reads as a slightly warm dark grey rather than true black.

**Accent / Highlight Colors:**
- `#C8FF00` / `#BEFF00` — Electric yellow-green (Anduril accent): used as a neon highlight accent on the Roadrunner-M product, visible on the carbon fiber tail markings in product photography. This is NOT used in UI but appears embedded in product design photography as a signature color.
- `#FF0000` / `#CC1B1B` — Red/U.S. flag red: appears in archival imagery (Uncle Sam poster, "Rebooting" editorial), in the "The Grind" editorial piece thumbnail, and in product photography showing Anduril insignia on vehicles. Used as patriotic/editorial accent, never as a UI element.
- `#1E6B8A` / `#0B4F6C` — Deep teal/ocean blue: the background tint used on the Dive-XL product hero — suggesting ocean depth. This is a domain-specific atmospheric color, approximately `#0D5F7A`.
- `#2F2F20` / `#3A3A28` — Dark olive-brown: visible in the opening homepage video frame (the extremely dark olive/brown of the factory floor at night).

**Interactive / UI Element Colors:**
- `#FFFFFF` with black text — Primary CTA button style: "Explore Open Roles" button on Careers page uses white fill with black text, no border-radius (sharp rectangular corners or very minimal radius ≤2px). Background is pure white, text is pure black.
- `#FFFFFF` with thin dark border — Search field on News page: white input area labeled "SEARCH" in uppercase tracking.
- Arrow/icon color: `#FFFFFF` on black backgrounds; `#000000` on white/light backgrounds. Down-arrow scroll indicator follows same rule.

**Navigation Colors:**
- Nav background: fully transparent (the nav sits over video/image heroes, making background content show through). When light pages load, the nav text switches to black against the light bg.
- Nav link text: `#FFFFFF` on dark backgrounds, `#000000` on light backgrounds — adaptive contrast.
- Logo: `#FFFFFF` on dark; `#000000` or `#1A1A0A` on light.

**Footer Colors:**
- Footer background: `#000000` pure black.
- Footer text: `#FFFFFF` for headings and links; slightly muted off-white for secondary footer text.
- Footer logo: `#FFFFFF` wordmark + symbol on black.

**404 Page:**
- Background: `#000000`.
- "404" numeral: `#FFFFFF`, enormous, right-aligned.
- Background animation: the Lattice mesh visualization in dark grey-on-black particle dots.
- Text: "Page not found" in white on the left, matching the product page typographic placement convention.

**Photography-Embedded Colors (not UI but visible on-screen):**
- Neon yellow-green accent stripes on Roadrunner-M carbon fiber fuselage: `#C8E000`–`#D4FF00`.
- Anduril blue/teal seen on underwater Dive-XL photography: deep aquamarine `#00627A`.
- American flag red/white/blue: present in editorial YFQ-44A photography and "Rebuild the Arsenal" article imagery.

### Key Observation on Palette Strategy

Anduril deploys what designers call a **binary restraint strategy**: the UI itself uses exactly three colors — black, white, and transparency — while the brand world introduces accent and domain color exclusively through product photography, environmental contexts, and editorial imagery. There are NO primary CTA colors in red, orange, or brand-specific chromatic hues. The UI makes no chromatic promises it cannot keep in classified or sensitive contexts.

The one strategic exception: **the Lattice platform** breaks from the black canvas to use a warm sage/mist background — an intentional system-level signal that says "this is software and intelligence, not kinetic hardware." It creates a visual hierarchy: hardware = black/dark; software/intelligence = light/sage.

### Color Application by Page Type

**Homepage:** Black hero with full-bleed video. Product card grid alternates dark imagery with minimal light — white text always. Footer: black. Editorial thumbnails introduce color through photography (olive khaki tones for patriotic Uncle Sam; cinematic American red-white-blue for "The Grind").

**Product Detail Pages (PDPs) — Hardware (Ghost, Roadrunner, Fury, Dive-XL, Space):** Consistent black backgrounds. White typography. Product photography on black creates near-zero separation — the product floats in void. Domain-specific atmospheric photography introduces subtle hue (teal for sea, neutral grey for land, black for air).

**Product/Platform Pages — Software (Lattice Mesh):** Sage/warm grey-white background (`~#DDDAD2`). Dark near-black text. Animated particle mesh in similar sage-grey with darker grey nodes. The color itself communicates intangibility, network, data fog.

**Editorial/Manifesto (Rebooting the Arsenal):** Pure white `#FFFFFF` background. Pure black `#000000` text. No color. Clinical academic severity. The only visual accent comes from embedded historical photography with sepia tones and archival coloration.

**News Index (Newsroom):** Same sage background as Lattice — warm off-white. Black typography. Full-bleed hero photo acts as color accent.

**News Article Detail:** White background. Black text. Minimal UI chrome. The article is the content; color comes only from embedded images.

**Careers:** Black hero. White type. CTA button is white-on-black page = white rectangle with black type.

**Open Roles:** Black hero with enormous white counter ("1760"). Below the fold: dark charcoal filter bar with uppercase category labels in white. Search field in near-black input.

**Arsenal-1 Landing:** Black hero. White type. Below fold: photography-driven modules.

**404 Error:** Black background, white "404" text, animated particle/mesh background referencing Lattice.

**Mission Page:** Sage/cream off-white background (`~#F0EDE6`). Black text. No accent colors.

### Color Hierarchy & Emotional Role Analysis

1. **Black** = stealth, military authority, classification, seriousness, night operations, advanced technology. It is the primary canvas. It says: "this product operates where light doesn't."
2. **White** = transparency, truth, the manifesto, the argument, the record. Used for long-form editorial to signal intellectual rigor and journalistic credibility.
3. **Sage/Mist (#DDDAD2)** = intelligence, software, networked cognition, the invisible layer. Used only for digital/software products to visually distinguish them from kinetic hardware.
4. **Photography-color** = humanity, geography, mission context, patriotism. Color only enters the system through real-world imagery — never through synthetic UI choices.

### Colors NOT Used (Deliberate Absences)

- No red CTAs or alert colors in the UI (despite the domain's life-or-death stakes — a remarkable restraint).
- No brand blue (the entire defense-industry default, e.g., Lockheed Martin, Raytheon, Boeing, DARPA — all use blue). Anduril's absence of blue is a strategic anti-establishment statement.
- No gradients in the UI layer (gradients appear only in environmental photography — the ocean in Dive-XL, sky in Fury photos).
- No orange (the Silicon Valley startup default — explicitly avoided).
- No green despite being a military/camouflage color.
- No purple, pink, or any "consumer tech" hues.
- No transparency/opacity effects on UI components (pure opaque black or white only).
- No color-coded category system (no "Sea = blue, Land = green, Air = grey" color taxonomy in the navigation or UI — a surprising absence that maintains visual unity across domains).

## 2. Typography

### Font Families Identified

**Primary Display / Wordmark Font:** A grotesque sans-serif, ultra-high weight (900 Black / Extra-Black), closely resembling **Neue Haas Grotesk Display Black** or possibly **GT America Expanded Black** or **Aktiv Grotesk Extended Black**. The letterforms have:
- Extremely tight optical tracking at large display sizes.
- Very slight condensed quality on some characters (the "G" in "Ghost" is notably wide).
- Single-story lowercase "a" and "g" visible in mixed-case product names.
- The overall texture is closest to **Söhne Breit Black** (Klim Type Foundry) or **Neue Haas Grotesk Display 95 Black** — a Swiss grotesque in its most extreme weight.
- Used for: Product name hero wordmarks (Ghost, Roadrunner, Fury, Space, Dive-XL, Arsenal-1, Mesh).

**Secondary Display / Headline Font:** The same grotesque family but in a lighter weight — approximately **Bold (700)** or **SemiBold (600)**. Used for:
- Section headings: "News & Insights," "Find Your Future at Anduril," "Transforming Defense Capabilities with Advanced Technology," "Rebooting the Arsenal of Democracy."
- Editorial section headings within long-form content.
- The font remains the same family but at a weight that allows readability at mid-scale sizes.

**Body / Paragraph Font:** The same sans-serif family continues into body copy at **Regular (400)** and **Medium (500)** weights. On article detail pages: body text in a book weight (~400). On product pages: the small descriptor paragraphs use the same typeface at Regular weight in all-caps with expanded tracking.

**Monospace/Code-style Labels:** A **narrow monospace** typeface is used for the bracket labels like `[AAV]`, `[XL-AUV]`, `[SPACE]`, `[A-1]`. These appear in square brackets, leftmost column, in very small size (~10-12px), high tracking. Closest match: **IBM Plex Mono** or a similar mono with a slightly mechanical, classified-document feel. These are the "classification codes" of each product.

**Category/Section Labels:** Small, all-caps, wide-tracked sans-serif — same family as the primary grotesque but at Regular weight with approximately +200–300ms letter-spacing. Used for: "NEWSROOM," "CAREERS," "MISSION," "LATTICE," "AUTONOMOUS AIR VEHICLE," "DESIGNED BY ANDURIL," "BUILT IN OHIO."

### Typography Application by Page Type

### Font Pairing Philosophy

The system uses a **single-family, multi-weight mono-typeface strategy** — the same grotesque family across every weight from Regular to Black, with hierarchy achieved entirely through:
1. Scale (200px hero vs 12px label)
2. Weight (Black vs Regular)
3. Case (ALL CAPS expanded for labels vs mixed case for editorial)
4. Color (white on dark vs dark on light)

This is a masterclass in typographic restraint. There is no serif used anywhere for contrast. There is no display typeface for ornamentation. The monospace/code-style bracket labels are the only departure, and they function as a system design pattern (classification codes) rather than a display font choice. Semiotically: one family across all applications says "we are one unified intelligence platform — everything speaks the same language." It mirrors Anduril's thesis that hardware, software, and autonomy must be integrated, not siloed.

### Weight Distribution Analysis

- **Black/900:** Product name heroes, editorial manifesto headings, large stat numerics. Reserved for maximum visual mass and brand moment.
- **Bold/700:** Section headings, article titles, benefit headings, feature subheadings. The workhorse weight for hierarchy below hero level.
- **Medium/500:** Navigation links, CTA button text, product specs, some microcopy labels. Subtle authority without heaviness.
- **Regular/400:** Body text, date stamps, bylines, footer links, article body copy. Neutral and readable.
- **Light/300:** Rarely if ever detected. Not visibly deployed in the primary UI — Anduril does not use thin/light weights as aesthetic softness.

The **absence of Light and Thin weights** is telling: this is a brand that does not do delicate. Every typographic moment is either invisible (Regular) or commanding (Black).

### Letter-spacing Rules

1. **Hero product names (Black weight, large scale):** Negative or zero tracking — approximately -1% to -3% at viewport-scale. Natural optical tightening at large sizes.
2. **ALL CAPS labels, category codes, section identifiers:** Expanded tracking — approximately +0.15em to +0.25em. Wide spacing at small sizes for legibility and officiousness.
3. **Navigation links:** Normal tracking (~0 to +0.05em).
4. **Body copy:** Normal tracking (0em). No micro-adjustments.
5. **CTA button text:** Slightly expanded when in ALL CAPS context (+0.05–0.1em); normal in mixed-case.

- ~12–14px: Date stamps, bylines, form labels, spec values

## 3. Logo & Wordmark

### Primary Logo Description

The Anduril logo is a **combined mark** consisting of:
1. A **geometric symbol/monogram** on the left: a stylized "A" formed by a triangular shape with an internal smaller triangle (or "eye" / chevron-within-triangle form). It reads as a stylized sword pommel, shield, or arrowhead — all simultaneously. The form has clean, hard-edged geometric construction with no curves — fully rectilinear/triangular. It is approximately 1:1 square proportion.
2. A **wordmark** "ANDURIL" in all-caps immediately to the right of the symbol, in the same grotesque family used site-wide, at approximately Regular-to-Medium weight and standard/slightly-expanded tracking.

### Logo Characteristics

- Typeface: Same grotesque sans-serif as body (NOT a custom logotype, or appears very close to the standard brand typeface).
- Weight: Medium/Regular — notably not Bold or Black. The wordmark maintains authority through all-caps formatting rather than weight.
- Case: ALL CAPS always.
- Tracking: Slightly expanded — approximately +0.05–0.1em.
- The symbol and wordmark sit on the same baseline; they are equal height.

### Placement Rules

- **Always top-left corner** of every page, in the navigation bar.
- The nav bar is positioned absolutely over the hero content (transparent background) — the logo floats over imagery.
- No background box, no pill, no card — the logo is raw, frameless, direct.
- Flanking elements: To the right, primary navigation links (Sea, Land, Air, Space, Lattice, Arsenal-1). Far right: Search and Company+.
- No tagline or descriptor accompanies the logo anywhere on the site.
- Logo size in nav: approximately 120–140px wide (symbol + wordmark). Compact — never ornamental.

### Color Variations Observed

1. **White version** (#FFFFFF): Used on all black/dark backgrounds — homepage, product pages, careers, open roles, 404, footer.
2. **Black version** (#000000 or near-black): Used on all light backgrounds — Mission page, Lattice Mesh page, editorial/manifesto, news pages.
3. **No color version detected** — no branded color logo (no olive, no yellow, no teal version used in UI).

### Sizing Details

- Navigation: Logo renders at approximately 32–36px height (symbol) + wordmark at matching height. The combined width is approximately 120–140px.
- Footer: Same logo at similar size on black background.
- No "small" or "favicon" versions visible in the UI chrome. The browser favicon/tab icon would be a simplified version of the triangle symbol.

### Brand Mark System

- **Combined mark primary:** Symbol + wordmark together (always used in navigation and footer).
- **Symbol alone:** The triangle/chevron mark appears standalone in watermark form on product photography (visible on the Dive-XL underwater photograph — "ANDURIL" wordmark + symbol branded directly onto the vehicle body in a white stencil-like treatment).
- **No monogram usage** detected (no single-letter "A").
- **No embossed/dimensional version** in UI — always flat.

### Logo Relationship to Brand Heritage/Positioning

"Anduril" is the name of the legendary reforged sword in Tolkien's Lord of the Rings — "Flame of the West," the blade of Aragorn, a sword of kings reforged from broken pieces into something greater and more powerful. The geometric mark is a sword/arrow/triangle — literal and symbolic simultaneously. The brand name and the mark together create an unmistakable mythology: **technology as the reforged weapon of democracy**. This is not an accidental naming — it is the brand strategy made literal. The geometric precision of the mark (no curves, hard angles, shield-like form) reinforces engineering rigor, not software-industry playfulness. It is the anti-Apple logo — no warmth, no organicism, no approachability by design.

## 4. Layout & Grid System

### Global Structure

- **Max content width:** Appears full-bleed on most pages — hero sections span 100vw. Content modules within light-background pages (Lattice, Mission, News) appear to use an internal max-width of approximately **1280–1440px** centered, with left-edge content starting at approximately **28–32px** from viewport left.
- **Left margin/gutter:** Consistent ~28–32px left margin for text content on all pages. Navigation logo also aligns to this left margin.
- **Header height:** Navigation bar approximately **52–60px** tall. Transparent background, positioned absolutely over hero content.
- **Section padding:** Vertical padding between major modules approximately **80–120px**.
- **Column structure:** Content adapts to a loose 12-column grid. Product spec tables use ~2-column (label left, value right). Editorial uses a ~50/50 or 60/40 split for two-column layouts.
- **Footer columns:** 4-column footer — left: logo (wide, ~40% of width); then COMPANY, WORK WITH US, SOCIAL in 3 adjacent columns of approximately equal width.

## 7. Photography Style

**Angles:**

- Side profiles dominating (especially on Ghost, Roadrunner, Dive-XL). 
- Top-down overhead for ground vehicles.
- Close-up macro for detail shots (part numbers, engine exhausts, fin edges, carbon weave).

**Props:** None. No human hands. No scale objects. The product is alone.

**Casting:** Engineers appear to be actual Anduril employees — diverse, technically-dressed (safety glasses, work wear, outdoor gear). No models. No "person staring thoughtfully at a laptop in a coffee shop." Subjects appear focused, purposeful, competent.

**Posing:** Action or candid-action — engineers looking at equipment, assembling hardware, operating devices in the field. No posed "look at camera" shots detected on careers/mission pages.

The art direction principle is: **photography as evidence, not aspiration.** Every image on the site exists to prove capability — to show the physical reality of the product, the literal environments of operation, the actual people building it.
